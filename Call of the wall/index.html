What is this?

A jump. A ritual. A whole lot of people who decided to start the day right.

Call of the Wall is the simplest tradition there is. Friday morning, the sun comes up over Little Traverse Bay, and a crowd of friends and strangers walks to the end of the breakwall in Petoskey, Michigan.

Then we jump. Into the cold, clear water of Lake Michigan. Together.

That's it. That's the whole thing. You come as you are, make a few friends, get a little braver, and start your weekend with a story. The wall is calling — and you must go.

How it works

Three steps. Zero experience needed.

01

Show up

Friday morning, 7am sharp, at the breakwall in Petoskey, MI. Bring a towel, something warm for after, and that's it.

02

Walk the wall

We head out to the end together — coffee optional, good company guaranteed. Strangers become friends by the time we reach the lighthouse.

03

Jump

Into Lake Michigan you go. Cold for a second, unforgettable forever. Towel off, and you've officially answered the call.

The official rules

Five rules. That's the whole rulebook.

1
Everyone is welcomeSeriously. All ages, all abilities, first-timers and regulars. If you showed up, you belong.
2
Swim at your own riskOpen water is open water. Know your limits and respect the lake.
3
Look out for each otherWe jump as a group and watch each other's backs. Always.
4
Lightning & water don't play wellRain, we jump. Lightning, we don't. Weather calls get made on the wall.
5
Make new friends, have funThat's the point of the whole thing. See you Friday morning.
